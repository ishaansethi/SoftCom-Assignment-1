Title
Netaji portrait in Rashtrapati Bhavan based on original picture: Sources

Link
https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/netaji-portrait-in-rashtrapati-bhavan-based-on-original-picture-sources/articleshow/80449124.cms

Date & Time
Jan 25, 2021, 17:04  IST

Text
TMC MP Mohua Moitra kicked off a controversy on Twitter after she claimed that a portrait ofNetaji Subhash Chandra Boseunveiled by PresidentRam Nath Kovindwas actually based on a still of an actor who portrayed the freedom fighter in a biopic.

However,Rashtrapati Bhavansources dismissed the allegation and said that the portrait is based on an actual image of Bose.



Moitra later deleted her tweet.



"We do not comment on tweets. The painting was made in Rashtrapati Bhavan and you think that it is a painting of an actor. What kind of question is it? Something is being run on Twitter, but it may be politically motivated. What can we say on it?," a senior Rashtrapati Bhavan official told news agnecy IANS on condition of anonymity.

The official replied to the question after the tweet went viral with some people alleging that the "President unveiled the official portrait of actorPrasenjit Chatterjee(Bumbada) and notNetajiat the Rashtrapati Bhavan".

The President on January 23 unveiled a portrait of Netaji at theRashtrapati Bhawanto mark the beginning of the year-long celebrations to commemorate the freedom fighter's 125th birth anniversary.

The government has decided to observe January 23 as "Parakram Divas" from now on every year. Bose was born in 1897.
